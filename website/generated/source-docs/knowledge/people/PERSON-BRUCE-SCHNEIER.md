# Bruce Schneier

## Summary

Bruce Schneier is an internationally renowned security technologist, cryptographer, and author who serves on the boards of the Electronic Frontier Foundation, AccessNow, and the Tor Project, and is a Fellow at Harvard's Berkman Klein Center for Internet and Society.

## Verified Facts

- Fellow at the Berkman Klein Center for Internet and Society at Harvard University.
- Lecturer in Public Policy at the Harvard Kennedy School.
- Board member of the Electronic Frontier Foundation.
- Board member of AccessNow.
- Advisory board member of the Electronic Privacy Information Center (EPIC) and VerifiedVoting.org.
- Author of over one dozen books including "Applied Cryptography" (a foundational cryptography text), "Data and Goliath", and "We Have Root".
- His newsletter "Crypto-Gram" and blog "Schneier on Security" are read by over 250,000 people.
- Called a "security guru" by The Economist.
- Described as one of the world's leading security technologists.
- Has authored hundreds of articles, essays, and academic papers on security, privacy, and cryptography.

## Historical Context

Schneier has been a central figure in public debate about cryptography, surveillance, and digital privacy since the 1990s. His book "Applied Cryptography" (1994) became a definitive reference for the field. He has consistently advocated for strong encryption, privacy rights, and against government backdoors in security systems. His position on the EFF board connects him directly to the legal defense of digital rights.

## Relevance to Open Source and Software Companies

Schneier's work informs the security practices that open source and commercial software companies rely on. His advocacy for strong encryption directly supports companies implementing cryptographic protections. His board roles at EFF and the Tor Project connect him to the legal and technical defense of software freedom. His public writing educates developers, policymakers, and the public about why security and privacy matter for software.
