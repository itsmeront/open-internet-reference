# Rand Paul

## Summary

Rand Paul is a U.S. Senator from Kentucky who has introduced legislation to protect Fourth Amendment rights in the digital age, including the Fourth Amendment Restoration and Protection Act and amendments to prevent government from compelling companies to weaken encryption.

## Verified Facts

- U.S. Senator from Kentucky.[^1]
- Introduced the Fourth Amendment Restoration and Protection Act.[^1]
- Co-introduced Aaron's Law with Wyden and Lofgren.[^2]
- Co-introduced the Fourth Amendment Is Not For Sale Act with Wyden.[^3]
- Co-introduced the USA RIGHTS Act.[^4]
- Proposed joint surveillance reform amendments with Wyden, including a prohibition on requiring companies to weaken encryption.[^5]
- Published an op-ed describing a FISA reform amendment to protect encryption and Fourth Amendment rights.[^6]

## Key Legislation

- **Fourth Amendment Restoration and Protection Act**: Legislation to restore constitutional Fourth Amendment protections in the digital age.
- **[Aaron's Law](../legal/STAT-CFAA-1030.md)** (co-sponsor): Bicameral CFAA reform with Wyden and Lofgren.
- **[Fourth Amendment Is Not For Sale Act](../../bibliography/people/SRC-WYDEN-FOURTH-AMENDMENT-NOT-FOR-SALE.md)**: Joint legislation with Wyden to prevent government purchase of data it would need a warrant to obtain.
- **[USA RIGHTS Act](../../bibliography/people/SRC-WYDEN-USA-RIGHTS-ACT.md)**: Reforms to Section 702 surveillance authorities.
- **[Anti-encryption-backdoor amendments](../../bibliography/people/SRC-WYDEN-PAUL-SURVEILLANCE-AMENDMENTS.md)**: Amendments to prevent government from compelling companies to weaken encryption or build backdoors.

## Historical Context

Paul has been a consistent advocate for civil liberties and privacy in the Senate, often working across party lines with Democrats like Wyden on surveillance reform and digital rights issues. His FISA and surveillance amendment work reflects a Fourth Amendment position that aligns with digital rights concerns about mass surveillance.

## Relevance to Open Source and Software Companies

Paul's anti-encryption-backdoor amendments directly protect software companies from being compelled to weaken their security implementations. His Fourth Amendment work protects user data from warrantless government access. His CFAA reform co-sponsorship supports security researchers and developers.
