# Janet Abbate

## Summary

Janet Abbate is a Virginia Tech professor of science, technology, and society and author of *Inventing the Internet*, the standard academic history of how ARPANET became the public Internet.

## Verified Facts

- Professor of Science, Technology, and Society at Virginia Tech; co-director of the STS graduate program in Northern Virginia.
- PhD from the University of Pennsylvania (1994).
- Author of *Inventing the Internet* (MIT Press, 1999), widely cited as the standard academic reference on Internet history.
- Author of *Recoding Gender: Women's Changing Participation in Computing* (MIT Press, 2012).
- Co-editor of *Standards Policy for Information Infrastructure* (MIT Press, 1995, with Brian Kahin).
- Research focuses on history, culture, and policy of the internet and computing, including the emergence of computer science as a discipline.

## Expert Testimony and Public Advocacy

Abbate provides independent historical context that participant witnesses cannot — reconstructing industry norms, institutional timelines, and policy debates from archival research rather than personal memory. Courts and regulators consult historians like Abbate when cases require establishing what was technically and socially "normal" during a specific period of internet development.

## Relevance to Lawsuits Involving Software and Internet Infrastructure

Abbate is particularly useful when a case involves:

- Historical evolution from ARPANET to commercial Internet
- Periodization: what "the internet" meant at a given date
- Gender and labor history of computing (relevant to workforce and discrimination claims)
- Standards and information-infrastructure policy in historical context
