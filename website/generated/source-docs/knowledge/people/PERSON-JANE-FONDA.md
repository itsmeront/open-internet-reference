# Jane Fonda

## Summary

Jane Fonda is an actor and activist who publicly relaunched the Committee for the First Amendment in October 2025. She framed the relaunch as a response to renewed government pressure on free expression, invoking the Committee her father Henry Fonda joined during the McCarthy era.

## Verified Facts

- The Committee for the First Amendment homepage's October 1, 2025 timeline entry identifies Jane Fonda as speaking at the Committee's relaunch about federal repression of political beliefs and the return of those forces.[^1]
- CNN reported on October 1, 2025 that Jane Fonda relaunched the Committee for the First Amendment and shared a letter asking Hollywood peers to join the relaunched committee.[^2]
- In that letter, Fonda wrote that she was relaunching the same Committee her father, Henry Fonda, joined with other artists during the McCarthy era.[^2]
- CNN reported that more than 550 Hollywood figures had signed on as supporters of the relaunched committee.[^2]
- A Committee spokesperson told CNN that Fonda reconvened the committee in response to attacks on free speech and hoped to present a united front against government censorship, intimidation, and fear.[^2]
