# Cory Booker

## Summary

Cory Booker is a U.S. Senator from New Jersey who has introduced the Banning Surveillance Advertising Act and co-sponsored bipartisan surveillance and civil-liberties reforms including the Fourth Amendment Is Not For Sale Act.

## Verified Facts

- U.S. Senator from New Jersey.[^1]
- Introduced the Banning Surveillance Advertising Act.[^1]
- The bill would prohibit advertising networks from using personal data for targeted ads, with limited location targeting exceptions.[^1]
- The bill allows contextual advertising based on the content a user is engaging with.[^1]
- Booker described surveillance advertising as a predatory and invasive practice.[^1]
- Co-sponsored the Fourth Amendment Is Not For Sale Act with Wyden and Paul.[^2]

## Key Legislation

- **[Banning Surveillance Advertising Act](../generated/source-docs/bibliography/people/SRC-BOOKER-SURVEILLANCE-ADVERTISING.md)**: Prohibits targeted advertising based on personal data and data-broker purchases while preserving contextual advertising.
- **Fourth Amendment Is Not For Sale Act** (co-sponsor): Joint legislation with Wyden and Paul to prevent warrantless government purchase of Americans' personal data from data brokers.

## Historical Context

Booker has worked across party lines on civil-liberties issues, including surveillance reform with Rand Paul and privacy protections against commercial data exploitation. His surveillance advertising bill targets the business model behind much of the modern ad-supported internet.

## Relevance to Open Source and Software Companies

Limits on surveillance advertising affect ad-supported platforms, analytics vendors, and any software company that monetizes user behavior data. Booker's work on data-broker restrictions aligns with broader efforts to reduce warrantless access to commercially collected data that software products generate.
