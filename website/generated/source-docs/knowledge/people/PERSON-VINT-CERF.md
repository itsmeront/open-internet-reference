# Vint Cerf

## Summary

Vint Cerf is a computer scientist widely recognized as a co-designer of TCP/IP, a former ICANN chairman and Internet Society founding president, and Google's Chief Internet Evangelist who frequently speaks on internet architecture, governance, and policy.

## Verified Facts

- Co-designer of the TCP/IP protocols and the architecture of the Internet, working with Robert Kahn.[^1]
- Vice President and Chief Internet Evangelist at Google.[^1]
- Founding president of the Internet Society (ISOC), 1992–1995.[^1]
- Chairman of the ICANN board, 2000–2007.[^1]
- Former executive at MCI, the Corporation for National Research Initiatives, and DARPA; former Stanford faculty member.[^1]
- Recipient of the ACM Turing Award (with Robert Kahn), Presidential Medal of Freedom, and National Medal of Technology.[^1]
- Visiting Scientist at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory; involved in interplanetary networking research.[^1]
- Past President of the Association for Computing Machinery; chairman of ARIN.[^1]

## Expert Testimony and Public Advocacy

Cerf is a frequent speaker before policymakers, industry forums, and the public on how the Internet works, why its design choices matter, and how regulation affects connectivity, security, and accessibility. His credibility rests on direct participation in the Internet's foundational engineering rather than litigation advocacy. Legal teams often seek experts with this profile to explain network architecture, naming and numbering, protocol behavior, and the technical feasibility of proposed remedies.

## Relevance to Lawsuits Involving Software and Internet Infrastructure

Cerf is particularly useful when a case turns on:

- How the Internet's layered architecture actually works
- DNS, routing, or protocol design questions
- Whether a proposed technical mandate is feasible or would break interoperability
- Historical context for Internet governance decisions (ICANN, address policy, standards development)

He complements attorneys and policy advocates by translating engineering reality for courts and regulators.
