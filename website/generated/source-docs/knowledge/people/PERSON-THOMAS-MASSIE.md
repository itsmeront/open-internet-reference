# Thomas Massie

## Summary

Thomas Massie is a U.S. Representative from Kentucky who has led bipartisan House amendments to block government-mandated encryption backdoors and limit surveillance cooperation that weakens security standards.

## Verified Facts

- U.S. Representative from Kentucky.[^1]
- Sponsored a bipartisan anti-surveillance amendment with Zoe Lofgren and Ted Poe.[^1]
- The amendment passed the House 383-43 as part of a Commerce, Justice, Science appropriations bill.[^1]
- The amendment would prevent NIST from cooperating with the NSA to weaken encryption standards for surveillance purposes.[^1]
- Massie stated that weakening encryption puts Americans' health records and financial information at risk.[^1]
- Massie, an MIT-trained engineer, has repeatedly offered amendments to block government-compelled encryption backdoors.[^1]

- **Anti-encryption backdoor amendments** (multiple years): House-passed amendments to prevent agencies from compelling companies to build surveillance backdoors into products.

## Historical Context

Massie brings an engineering background to encryption policy debates and has worked across party lines with Zoe Lofgren on surveillance-limiting amendments. Several of his House-passed privacy amendments were later stripped from omnibus spending bills, but the recorded votes document congressional support for encryption protections.

## Relevance to Open Source and Software Companies

Massie's amendments target government efforts to weaken cryptographic standards and compel backdoors in commercial products. That directly affects software companies, security researchers, and open source projects implementing encryption.
