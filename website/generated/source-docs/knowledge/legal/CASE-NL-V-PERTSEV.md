# Netherlands v. Pertsev (Tornado Cash)

## Summary

East Brabant District Court judgment ECLI:NL:RBOBR:2024:2069 (May 14, 2024) convicted a Tornado Cash developer of money laundering and imposed a prison sentence of 64 months. An official English judiciary news page identifies the defendant as Alexey Pertsev.

## Verified Facts

- The judgment is published as ECLI:NL:RBOBR:2024:2069 by Rechtbank Oost-Brabant; the decision date is May 14, 2024; the case number is 82/198261-22.[^1]
- The court’s contents abstract states conviction for money laundering of large quantities of cryptocurrency, that the defendant developed Tornado Cash with co-perpetrators, and that the court imposed a prison sentence of 64 months.[^1]
- The charged period in the indictment as described in the judgment includes activity from July 9, 2019 through August 10, 2022, alleging habitual money laundering involving approximately 535,809 ETH valued at approximately $1,217,343,820, while knowing or reasonably suspecting criminal origin.[^1]
- The judgment states Tornado Cash was developed so that it automatically performed acts essential to money laundering, combined maximum anonymity and concealment techniques with a serious lack of functionalities enabling identification, control, or investigation, and that the defendant continued developing and exploiting Tornado Cash while disregarding abuse occurring through it.[^1]
- Official English judiciary news states that a developer of Tornado Cash received a jail sentence for laundering billions of dollars in cryptocurrency and identifies Alexey Pertsev in that coverage.[^2]
- U.S. Treasury’s August 23, 2023 press release states that a third co-founder of Tornado Cash, Alexey Pertsev, was arrested on related money-laundering charges in the Netherlands in August 2022 by Dutch law enforcement authorities.[^3]

## Historical Context

Dutch authorities opened a criminal investigation (referred to in the judgment as Kidwelly) after reports that Tornado Cash was used to conceal proceeds of digital thefts. The East Brabant District Court issued its first-instance judgment on May 14, 2024. Parallel U.S. criminal proceedings against other alleged co-founders proceeded separately.

## Legal Analysis

Jurisdiction: Netherlands, East Brabant District Court (first instance). Authority level: first-instance criminal judgment under Dutch law. The court’s reasoning addresses developer liability for a mixing protocol under Dutch money-laundering law and is not a U.S. holding. Appeal status requires separate verification.
