# 17 U.S.C. § 107

## Summary

17 U.S.C. § 107 is the U.S. fair use statute, limiting copyright owners' exclusive rights for purposes such as criticism, comment, news reporting, teaching, scholarship, or research.

## Verified Facts

- The statute is codified at 17 U.S.C. § 107.[^1]
- Fair use of a copyrighted work for purposes such as criticism, comment, news reporting, teaching (including multiple copies for classroom use), scholarship, or research is not an infringement of copyright.[^1]
- Courts determine fair use by considering four non-exclusive factors: (1) purpose and character of the use, including commercial vs. nonprofit educational character; (2) nature of the copyrighted work; (3) amount and substantiality of the portion used in relation to the work as a whole; and (4) effect on the potential market for or value of the copyrighted work.[^1]
- The fact that a work is unpublished does not by itself bar a finding of fair use if the other factors support that finding.[^1]
- The EFF legal guide states that there are no hard-and-fast rules for fair use and that word-count or percentage guidelines are not the law.[^3]
