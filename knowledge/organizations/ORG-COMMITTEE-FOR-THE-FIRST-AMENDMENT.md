# Committee for the First Amendment

## Summary

The Committee for the First Amendment is an entertainment-industry coalition opposing government censorship and intimidation of critics in media, academia, and the arts. It was originally formed during the McCarthy era and was publicly relaunched in October 2025, led by Jane Fonda.

## Verified Facts

- The official homepage states the Committee was initially created during the McCarthy era, when the federal government repressed Americans for political beliefs, and that it is organizing again in response to a renewed campaign to silence critics in government, media, the judiciary, academia, and entertainment.[^1]
- The homepage publishes a joint statement affirming that free speech and free expression are inalienable rights and that the ability to criticize, question, protest, and mock those in power is foundational to American democracy.[^1]
- The homepage maintains an "On the Record" timeline documenting press-freedom and creative-expression events from late 2024 through mid-2026, including an October 1, 2025 entry for the Committee's relaunch featuring Jane Fonda.[^1]
- The official join page invites entertainment-industry colleagues to sign the Committee's statement and states the group is organizing inside the entertainment industry as the original Committee did.[^2]
- CNN reported on October 1, 2025 that Jane Fonda relaunched the Committee for the First Amendment and that more than 550 Hollywood figures had signed on as supporters.[^3]
- CNN reported that the Committee was first created in 1947 during the McCarthy era to defend free speech and oppose government activity, and that Henry Fonda and other Hollywood figures were among its original members.[^3]
- A Committee spokesperson told CNN that Fonda reconvened the group in response to attacks on free speech and hoped to present a united front against government censorship, intimidation, and fear.[^3]
