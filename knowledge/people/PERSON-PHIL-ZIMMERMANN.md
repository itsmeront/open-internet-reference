# Phil Zimmermann

## Summary

Phil Zimmermann is the creator of Pretty Good Privacy (PGP) whose 1990s export investigation made him a central figure in the legal fight over civilian access to strong cryptography.

## Verified Facts

- Creator of Pretty Good Privacy (PGP), published in 1991 as a human-rights-oriented encryption tool.
- Subject of a multi-year U.S. criminal investigation related to cryptographic software export; investigation closed without indictment in 1996.
- Internet Hall of Fame inductee (2012).
- EFF Pioneer Award recipient (1995).
- Developer of the ZRTP secure telephony protocol; co-founder of Silent Circle.
- Author of the Official PGP User's Guide (MIT Press, 1995).

## Expert Testimony and Public Advocacy

Zimmermann's personal history intersects directly with litigation-shaped cryptography policy: the government's attempt to treat widely distributed encryption software as munitions, and the community response that helped establish practical civilian crypto. He continues to speak on secure communications, surveillance, and the social importance of end-to-end encryption.

## Relevance to Lawsuits Involving Software and Internet Infrastructure

Zimmermann is particularly useful when a case involves:

- Whether encryption software qualifies as protected expression or export-controlled technology
- End-to-end encryption design and key custody models
- Historical precedent for criminal or regulatory pressure on crypto developers
- Secure messaging and VoIP architecture in liability or compelled-access disputes

His experience complements attorneys working on cases adjacent to Bernstein, DMCA anti-circumvention, and modern E2EE policy fights.
