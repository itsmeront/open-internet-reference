# Jonathan Zittrain

## Summary

Jonathan Zittrain is the George Bemis Professor of International Law at Harvard Law School, co-founder of the Berkman Klein Center, and a professor of computer science who is a leading public voice on internet law, platform governance, and digital technology policy.

## Verified Facts

- George Bemis Professor of International Law at Harvard Law School.[^1]
- Professor of Public Policy at the Harvard Kennedy School.[^1]
- Professor of Computer Science at the Harvard John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences.[^1]
- Faculty director of the Harvard Law School Library.[^1]
- Co-founder and director of Harvard's Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society.[^1]
- Author of *The Future of the Internet — And How to Stop It* (Yale University Press).[^1]
- Previously Professor of Internet Governance and Regulation at the Oxford Internet Institute.[^1]


[^1]: `SRC-ZITTRAIN-HLS` — Jonathan L. Zittrain Harvard Law School Faculty Profile. Official source; review reliability tier.

## Expert Testimony and Public Advocacy

Zittrain regularly contributes to public debate on internet regulation, intermediary liability, generative AI governance, and the incentives shaping major platforms. His cross-disciplinary appointments (law, policy, and computer science) make him a common reference point for explaining how technical systems interact with legal rules — a core need in technology litigation.

## Relevance to Lawsuits Involving Software and Internet Infrastructure

Zittrain is particularly useful when a case involves:

- Platform liability, content moderation, or intermediary safe harbors
- Generative AI governance and liability allocation
- "Generative vs. sterile" technology design and its legal implications
- Free expression, access control, and the social contract of online services

He bridges legal doctrine and technical architecture in ways that help courts understand second-order effects of remedies on software ecosystems.
