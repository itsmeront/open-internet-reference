# Cindy Cohn

## Summary

Cindy Cohn is an American civil liberties attorney specializing in internet law who served as executive director of the Electronic Frontier Foundation and was lead attorney in Bernstein v. DOJ, the landmark First Amendment challenge to cryptography export restrictions.

## Verified Facts

- The official EFF biography states Cohn first became involved with EFF in 1993, when EFF asked her to serve as the outside lead attorney in Bernstein v. Dept. of Justice.
- Bernstein v. DOJ was the successful First Amendment challenge to U.S. export restrictions on cryptography.
- Cohn became EFF's legal director in 2000.
- As legal director, she spearheaded EFF's lawsuits challenging the NSA's mass surveillance programs.
- She has been named one of the 100 most influential lawyers in America.
- Cohn is a graduate of the University of Michigan Law School.
- Prior to joining EFF, she was a civil litigator in private practice handling technology-related cases for 10 years.
- She is the author of "Privacy's Defender" published by MIT Press in March 2026.

## Relevance to Open Source and Software Companies

Cohn's successful litigation in Bernstein v. DOJ established that source code is protected speech under the First Amendment, directly benefiting all software companies and developers. Her decades of work on digital civil liberties, anti-surveillance litigation, and internet law make her one of the most significant attorneys in the field of software rights.
