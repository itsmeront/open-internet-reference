## How to Engage

1. **For policy engagement**: CDT works on policy advocacy; companies can engage with their research and policy teams on technology regulation.
2. **For amicus support**: CDT files amicus briefs in cases involving internet freedom, privacy, and free expression — companies facing relevant litigation may request CDT's involvement.
3. **Scope**: CDT works on:
   - Internet freedom and free expression
   - Privacy and surveillance
   - Government transparency
   - Platform governance
   - AI governance and accountability
4. **Note**: CDT is a policy organization rather than a legal services provider. They shape the regulatory environment through advocacy, research, and amicus participation.

## Scope

CDT is relevant to software companies navigating technology regulation, privacy law compliance, content moderation requirements, or government surveillance demands. Their policy work helps shape the legal environment, and their amicus participation can support companies in relevant litigation.
